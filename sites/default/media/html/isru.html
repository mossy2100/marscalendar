ISRU (in situ resource utilisation) is of fundamental importance to the success of any space settlement, primarily due to the cost and time associated with space transportation. Shipping supplies from Earth to anywhere in space is currently expensive due to Earth's gravity well and our current level of technological development with regards to space vehicles. At present there are no other locations from where supplies could be shipped.

Luna has an advantage over Mars that reduces the pressure to be as self-sufficient as possible, which is that Luna is close to Earth; and, what's more, it's always close, which means supplies can be shipped to Luna on relatively short notice at any time. In the case of Mars, however, supplies can only be sent every 26 months, when the Earth and Mars are aligned in their orbits. In addition, while we remain in a chemical propulsion paradigm the transfer times to Mars for cargo are on the order of 4-9 months, which is rather a long time to wait for a critical delivery.

Relying on Earth for critical resources such as air, water, food and basic materials will make any space settlement vulnerable, in much the same way that a Terran nation that needs to import food, oil or steel is vulnerable. Resource shortages, price rises, conflict, or issues with transportation systems could interrupt imports. A space settlement relying on Earth for critical resources would be vulnerable to shortages on Earth, lack of vehicles, war, natural disasters, economic downturn, and so on.

If a settlement is lacking in critical resources, it could fail. For example, if it runs low on energy, then the ECLSS could fail; if it runs low on food, people could starve; if it runs low on spare parts or the materials to make them, then equipment failure could also mean serious problems.

For maximising each settlement's survivability, it is therefore necessary for us to able to produce all, or almost all, of the resources we need from the local environment; especially critical resources such as energy, air, water and food.

As space transportation becomes cheaper and faster, ISRU will become less important, and space colonies will participate in trade (imports and exports) much as Terran nations do today.

There are two basic categories of resources that we need to consider:

  1. Fundamentals. These are the most important, and the ability to produce them from local resources must be developed as early as possible. They are:
    1. Energy
    2. Propellant
    3. Air
    4. Water
    5. Food
  2. Materials. While some spare parts can come from Earth, basic materials are necessary for repair and expansion of the settlement. The emphasis with regards to ISRU, in the near term, will be on materials that are relatively easy to produce, and represent a high mass fraction of the settlement. For example:
    1. Steel
    2. Concrete
    3. Glass
    4. Bricks
    5. Plastic
    6. Useful plants such as hemp and bamboo
